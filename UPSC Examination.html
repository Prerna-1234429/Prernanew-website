UPSC Examination

Your gateway to Civil Services

Overview

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE) every year to recruit candidates for various civil services positions such as IAS, IPS, and IFS. It is one of the most prestigious and competitive exams in India.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the UPSC CSE, candidates must fulfill the following requirements:

  • Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen.
  • Age Limit: Between 21 to 32 years (age relaxations apply for reserved categories).
  • Educational Qualification: A bachelorâ€™s degree from a recognized university.
  • Attempts: General category candidates can attempt the exam 6 times; OBC candidates have 9 attempts, while SC/ST candidates have unlimited attempts within the age limit.

Syllabus

The UPSC CSE has a comprehensive syllabus covering topics such as:

  • General Studies (History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Environment, and Current Affairs)
  • Optional Subject (Candidates can choose one optional subject from a list provided by UPSC)
  • Essay Writing
  • Language Papers (English and a regional language)

Exam Pattern

The UPSC CSE is conducted in three stages:

  • Preliminary Exam: Objective-type paper to test general knowledge and aptitude.
  • Main Exam: Descriptive-type papers, including General Studies, optional subject, and essay writing.
  • Interview: Final round to assess personality, communication skills, and suitability for civil services.
