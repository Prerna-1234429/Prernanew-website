SSC Examination

Your pathway to government jobs in India

Overview

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) conducts various competitive exams to recruit candidates for various positions in government departments and ministries. Some popular exams conducted by SSC include SSC CGL, SSC CHSL, SSC JE, SSC MTS, and SSC CPO.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for SSC exams, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen or as specified by SSC for specific categories.
  • Age Limit: Typically between 18 and 32 years (varies based on the exam and category).
  • Educational Qualification: Minimum 10+2 or graduation, depending on the exam type.

Syllabus

The syllabus for SSC exams varies based on the type of examination. Generally, the following topics are covered:

  • General Intelligence and Reasoning
  • Quantitative Aptitude
  • General Awareness
  • English Language and Comprehension
  • Technical subjects for specific exams like SSC JE

Exam Pattern

The SSC exams generally follow a multi-stage pattern:

  • Tier 1: Objective-type exam, covering general awareness, reasoning, and quantitative aptitude.
  • Tier 2: Subject-specific papers, with additional sections based on the position.
  • Tier 3: Descriptive-type paper to test writing skills (applicable to certain exams like SSC CGL).
  • Tier 4: Skill test or typing test (for specific exams and posts).
